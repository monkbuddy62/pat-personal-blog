How did I not know about github pages

April 24 — thoughts

You know its 2026 and my experience with how the web works, making webpages, and actually getting my hands dirty and trying shit is really only recently taking off. I attribute most of it to AI honestly. A lot of people are talking about how using AI is cheating, and I get that, I even agree with it in many cases. But if im completely honest, there are so many things I wanted to be able to make (like this blog) but I didnt have the hard skills to be able to code it. Nor did I have the motivation to learn. I do not use AI to write any of the posts, that would be weird, gross, and pointless. but using ai to create the layout and structure? who cares. Without it I would never have my site up. Without it I would still be trying to figure out how to get a theme to function properly while banging my head against the wall in my half broken wordpress blog. But right now I spun up a personal blog in less than an hour, own all the assets, pushed to github, and its hosted on pages for free? like what? i didnt even know it was possible.

So heres to a year of learning about a lot of technologies without actually having to grind through them, am I actually learning things though? or is it just the illusion of learning? I don't care, i have a larger access to things and am making tools I need, automating things, and have a higher success rate going from idea to thing that exists than I have before. (at least in terms of computer related projects).
